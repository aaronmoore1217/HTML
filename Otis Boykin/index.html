Otis Boykin, The Innovator that didn't let up

  Otis Boykin was born on August 20th, in Dallas, Texas. His father was a carpenter and his mother was a maid, who died when Otis was only a year old. The cause of his mothers death was heart failure, which inspired him to make one of the biggest improvements to the pacemaker, which allowed it to be much more accessible to people.

He was a valedictorian at his highschool, from which he graduated in 1938, then went to Fisk University and graduated in 1941. He started working as a laboratory assistant, testing automatic controls for airplanes. in 1944 became a full researcher at P.J. Nilsen Research Labs, shortly after, he created his own company, Boykin-Fruth Inc. He pursued graduate studies at the Illinois Institute of Technology from 1946 to 1947, but had to drop out due to not being able to afford tuition, but he didn't lose any motivation.

He began working hard on his own, working on inventions and innovations, with a special interest in electronics. Some of his first creations were variations of commonly used resistors, which were used to limit electric flow. Eventually he earned over 25 patents, some of which still have a lasting impact to this day.

Otis boykin passed away in Illinois, of heart disease.
